Weight loss & Weight Gain

Weight loss occurs by burning more calories than you consume, while weight gain happens when you consume more calories than you burn. Both processes are governed by the balance of energy intake (calories from food and drink) and energy expenditure (calories burned through physical activity and daily living). Lifestyle, genetics, environment, and certain health conditions can influence this balance, making healthy weight management a combination of diet and exercise

Let's Kickstart Your Workouts



Burn more calories than you eat: This is achieved by reducing your calorie intake through a healthy diet and increasing
your calorie expenditure through physical activity.

Limit portion sizes and choose healthier alternatives.


Stay physically active, and set realistic goals, like taking short walks.


Consume more calories than you burn: When your body takes in more energy than it uses, the excess is stored as body fat.


Focus on consuming healthy calories through a balanced diet.


Increase your intake of healthy fats, lean proteins, and whole grains

2. Environment

3. Genetics
